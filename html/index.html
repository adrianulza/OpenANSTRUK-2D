Who we are

Our mission
is to make
structural engineering
applications
accessible to
everyone.

Structural engineering software has always been exclusive. The barrier isn't knowledge, but, access. Industrial-grade tools are costly. Open-source alternatives require extensive coding skills most structural engineers don't have. Students graduate without ever using professional analysis software. Academics struggle to teach practical examples because institutional subscriptions are unaffordable. Researchers face steep learning curves for script-based tools or find themselves locked into proprietary softwares. Small firms and young engineers carry licensing costs as a burden while fighting to survive project by project.

Engineering principles are open. Published in textbooks, research articles, grounded in mathematics anyone can verify. Yet the tools that apply these principles remain gatekept: intuitive and industrial-ready, but costly; open and sophisticated, but requiring extensive coding knowledge; free but limited in capability.

We're building OpenAnstruk as an inclusive structural engineering software: open source, accessible, and industrial-grade. Designed for students learning the fundamentals, academics demonstrating practical examples, researchers exploring new methods, and engineers solving problems that matter.

We're not fighting the status quo. We're honoring a legacy that is one shaped across millennia by structural engineers whose work built the world we inhabit today. That legacy deserves tools everyone can access.
